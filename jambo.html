Jambo! Case Study

About Jambo!

Jambo! is a tech-food start-up based in London and founded by Julian Owusu. Jambo!'s goal is to popularize African food and put hundreds of family restaurants on the map by providing a unique delivery service focused on african cuisine.

The Brief

Create high fidelity mockups for both desktop and mobile in order to expand on Jambo!'s excisting brand identity as well as using the visuals for pitch deck presentations.

Challenges

As my first approach to UX and UI design the challenges were many. At the time I wasn't deeply familiar with the UX process. For this reason I approached the project mostly from a visual standpoint without paying much attention to user research, wireframes and low fidelity mockups among other things.

Although the results could have been richer if more attention had been paid to those areas, the end results were nonetheless satisfactory due to the nature of the task.

As I looked at other apps for guidance I discover another challenge:
How to walk the fine line between not being derivative but also not “reinventing the wheel”.

The Solution

I strove to create a crisp look that would grab people’s attention while proposing an intuitive and easy-to-use layout.


Jambo!'s main web page & restaurant screen on mobile.

In order to achieve this I looked at other similar apps for reference paying close attention to their content, layout and visuals. I took all of these things into consideration and designed the mockups with Jambo!'s excisting branding in mind.


Restaurant selection desktop page & mobile checkout screen.

Process

As I previously mentioned, this being my very first UX/UI project, I didn't follow the typical UX process. My focus was put on details and visuals almost exclusively.

When it came to the mobile screens, which I designed first, I began by doing research on similar apps and apps in general in order to get a better understanding on their information architecture and how they are structured.

I worked on Sketch, designing high fidelity mockups from the get go and going back to check already excisting apps while making changes and polishing the outcomes.

For the website mockups I followed a similar process, however, this time I had to make sure to convey clearly that this version of the website wasn't meant for delivery, but takeaway only.


"Takeaway" next to the logo and progress map aim to make sure the user understands their food won't be delivered to them.

Next Steps

Jambo! is currently in its early stages. As funding moves forward the development of both its website and mobile apps will continue.
This will be a great opportunity to revisit and rething the mockups created while also fully applying the UX process.
